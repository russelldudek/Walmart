Independent candidate vision for Walmart

Make every AI investment scannable.

Actual mandate

Turn fragmented AI demand across Legal, Compliance, Ethics, Investigations, and Security into a governed portfolio that produces adopted workflows, defensible decisions, reusable controls, and measurable value.

Company moment

Walmart is advancing agentic shopping, AI-powered associate tools, customer support, partner-based commerce, and AI-enabled operations. The governance function must enable speed while protecting trust, authority, and global obligations.

Central tensions

  • Speed vs. assurance
  • Global consistency vs. local legal judgment
  • Experimentation vs. portfolio discipline
  • Automation vs. human authority
  • Executive ambition vs. frontline adoption

White-space thesis

Govern AI as explicit decision contracts. Every use case should expose its outcome, legal basis, risk, data boundaries, human authority, operating owner, and evidence threshold before it earns funding or scale.

Executive discussion agenda

Questions for Walmart leadership

  1. Which governance decisions currently take too long, and which delays are prudent?
  2. Where does AI demand enter, and who has authority to fund, release, constrain, or stop it?
  3. Which controls should become reusable enterprise capabilities?
  4. What evidence would increase investment? What evidence should trigger a stop?
  5. Where has adoption failed even when the technology worked?
  6. How should global standards preserve local legal judgment?
  7. What should the CLO see monthly that is not visible today?

Success measures

Decision latency; realized value; control evidence; sustained workflow adoption; reuse of shared capabilities; exception quality; decision changes caused by evidence; and retirement of low-value work.
