Independent candidate vision for Walmart

Earn the right to standardize.

A tactical-empathy-first entry plan for Global Governance Digital Strategy.

Days 1-30 - Listen and map

Outcome

A shared map of decisions, work, authority, and friction.

  • Meet CLO staff, Governance leaders, Technology, Finance, Architecture, Privacy, Security, and delivery teams.
  • Inventory active and proposed AI use cases, spend, dependencies, vendors, controls, and owners.
  • Map current intake, prioritization, approval, exception, escalation, release, and retirement decisions.
  • Shadow representative workflows across Legal, Compliance, Ethics, Investigations, and Security.
  • Baseline decision latency, rework, adoption, evidence quality, and recurring bottlenecks.

Days 31-60 - Make decisions visible

Outcome

A minimum viable governance portfolio system.

  • Co-design the seven-clause Scannable AI Contract.
  • Define risk-sensitive stage gates and decision rights.
  • Create a single portfolio view with sponsor, owner, value thesis, control posture, evidence, and next decision.
  • Align Finance on investment and value-realization logic.
  • Select two contrasting use cases for controlled proof.

Entry principles

  • Do not import a framework before understanding what the current system protects.
  • Separate prudent review from avoidable queue time.
  • Preserve substantive governance authority.
  • Make assumptions and evidence visible.
  • Use real workflows, not abstract maturity scoring.
  • Stop low-value work early and respectfully.

Discovery artifacts

  • Decision-rights map
  • Stakeholder topology
  • Portfolio and spend inventory
  • Use-case risk segmentation
  • Workflow friction map
  • Control reuse map
  • Adoption burden map
  • Baseline scorecard

Early trust signals

Clear listening notes, visible decision follow-through, transparent assumptions, disciplined meeting load, and quick removal of one avoidable operating friction.

Days 61-90 - Run controlled proof

Outcome

Evidence that the mechanism improves decisions, not just documentation.

  • Apply the contract to two contrasting use cases with different risk and data profiles.
  • Test evaluation, logging, monitoring, escalation, and human-review mechanisms.
  • Measure decision latency, rework, adoption burden, and evidence completeness.
  • Run an executive portfolio review that results in a real Fund, Shape, Hold, or Stop decision.
  • Capture reusable controls and unresolved exceptions.

Days 91-120 - Scale the mechanism

Outcome

A two-quarter roadmap tied to value, trust, talent, and reuse.

  • Publish reusable decision, control, evaluation, and ownership patterns.
  • Align capacity and talent to the prioritized roadmap.
  • Define executive cadence, escalation paths, and portfolio health indicators.
  • Set value realization and adoption reviews with Finance and business owners.
  • Establish a learning backlog: what to standardize, localize, automate, or stop.

Review questions

  • Did evidence change a real decision?
  • Did the mechanism reduce ambiguity or merely add paperwork?
  • Are human decision rights clearer?
  • Did adoption burden become visible?
  • Can any control or evaluation pattern be reused?
  • Which work should stop?

Success at day 120

Leadership can see the portfolio, understand the next decision for every material initiative, distinguish value from activity, and scale governance expertise through a small number of trusted operating mechanisms.
