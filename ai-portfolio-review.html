Executive AI Portfolio Review

A decision meeting, not a status recital. Illustrative structure for discussion.

Meeting output

Every material use case leaves with a named posture - Fund, Shape, Hold, or Stop - plus an owner, next evidence requirement, and decision date.

1. Portfolio pulse

QuestionEvidenceDecision
Where is demand growing?Use-case intake by function, risk, value thesis, and reuse potentialCapacity allocation
Where is work waiting?Decision latency segmented by prudent review vs. avoidable queue timeRemove friction or preserve control
Where is value real?Baseline, realized outcome, adoption, and operating costFund, scale, or stop
Where is trust weak?Evaluation, data, authority, monitoring, exception, and owner gapsShape or hold

Decision discipline and follow-through

3. Posture rules

PostureRequired executive statement
FundThe outcome, authority, owner, controls, and evidence justify investment.
ShapeThe opportunity is credible; these clauses must be strengthened before release or scale.
HoldAmbiguity in data, authority, ownership, or evaluation is too material to proceed.
StopThe use case lacks a defensible basis, controllable risk profile, or sufficient value.

4. Monthly learning questions

  1. Which decision changed because evidence improved?
  2. Which control should become reusable?
  3. Where did adoption behavior contradict the business case?
  4. Which exception reveals a policy, architecture, or ownership gap?
  5. Which initiative should stop to release capacity?

5. Executive scorecard

MeasureInterpretation
Decision latencySpeed, segmented by risk and complexity
Realized outcomeVerified impact against baseline
Control evidenceQuality and coverage of assurance
Workflow adoptionSustained intended use and exceptions
Reuse ratioShared capability vs. duplicated work
Evidence-driven changeDecisions altered by learning

6. Close the meeting

For every reviewed initiative, record:

  • Posture and rationale
  • Named accountable owner
  • Next evidence requirement
  • Human decision boundary
  • Control or architecture dependency
  • Funding or capacity implication
  • Next decision date

Anti-bureaucracy test

If the review does not change funding, design, release, ownership, escalation, or retirement, it is likely reporting activity rather than governance.
